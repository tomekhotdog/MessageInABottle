# Document 43: Schedule of the goods shipped by William Mery, grocer, in the hoya of Adrian Johnson of Antwerp

**Section III:** Losses in the ship of Adrian Johnson (1533): English losses

## Summary

William Mery, a grocer, records the goods he shipped aboard Adrian Johnson's vessel: pepper, currants, and cinnamon. He notes he received only half his cinnamon (18 of 36 lb.) and provides itemised valuations in Flemish money totalling £118 4s 9d.

## Modern English Translation

Shipped by me, William Mery, grocer, under this mark [merchant's mark no. 1 in the left margin], in the vessel of Adrian Johnson of Antwerp:

One dry pipe [Note: a large cask] containing pepper, 2 bags weighing 519 lb. at 27d per lb. Flemish money. Sum: £58 7s 9d

Currants: 3 butts [Note: large barrels] weighing 4,514 lb. at 29s per hundredweight, Flemish money. Sum: £54 3s

Cinnamon: Note — 1 small bag weighing 36 lb., of which I have received only 18 lb. It cost 6s 4d per lb. Flemish. Sum of the half: £5 14s

Total sum: £118 4s 9d
